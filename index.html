Abstract

Panoramic shelf images are fundamental to retail analytics, yet constructing them remains a persistent challenge due to repetitive product patterns that confound stitching methods based on local feature matches. We propose a product-level image stitching pipeline that replaces ambiguous low-level feature matching with semantic product correspondences. A YOLOv11l detector trained on the SKU-110K dataset localizes products on individual frames, while DINOv3 embeddings serve as semantic descriptors for a fine-tuned LightGlue matcher. This detect-then-match approach elegantly avoids the detection quality degradation inherent to local feature matching pipelines. Pairwise correspondences are seamlessly extended to full panorama construction via a graph-based multi-image stitching framework that naturally partitions discontinuous sequences to maintain geometric integrity. We evaluate three DINOv3 variants, demonstrating that our semantic approach consistently outperforms baselines relying on local feature matching, with the ViT-B/16 variant achieving an end-to-end stitching F1 score of 88.4%. Furthermore, by drastically reducing the keypoint search space to semantic product instances, our pipeline achieves a remarkable 20× overall runtime speedup compared to state-of-the-art dense matchers, and a 7× speedup over sparse baselines.

Method Overview

RetailGlue inverts the conventional detect-after-stitch paradigm. Rather than applying detection to large, potentially distorted panoramas, we detect products on individual frames first and let those semantic identities drive the matching process.

Contributions

  • RetailGlue A novel product-level LightGlue adaptation using DINOv3 semantic embeddings as descriptors, specifically designed for robust retail image stitching.
  • Pipeline An inverted detect-then-match pipeline that preserves object detection quality by operating on individual frames rather than large stitched panoramas.
  • Graph A graph-based multi-image stitching framework with robustness to discontinuous captures via graph partitioning, achieving up to a 20× runtime speedup.
  • Dataset RemRetail100_v2: an extended benchmark with 32,958 annotated product correspondences across 919 image pairs.
